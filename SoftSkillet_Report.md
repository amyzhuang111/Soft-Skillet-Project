## Part 2: Event Playbook — Maximizing Impact at Trade Shows

### Pre-Event

**1. Build a Target List (2 weeks out)**
- Pull the attendee/exhibitor list and cross-reference against your ICP (independent restaurants, multi-unit groups, operators dissatisfied with OpenTable/Resy).
- Tier prospects: Tier 1 (enterprise groups, known pain), Tier 2 (mid-market, likely fit), Tier 3 (walk-ups, unknown).
- For Tier 1 accounts, research their current tech stack, recent press, and any existing Soft Skillet touchpoints (e.g., review app users).

**2. Pre-Book Meetings**
- Send personalized outreach to Tier 1 prospects 1-2 weeks before the show offering a 15-minute booth meeting at a specific time.
- Even 3-4 pre-booked meetings per day dramatically increases event ROI vs. relying entirely on walk-up traffic.

**3. Pitch Certification**
- Every rep should be able to deliver all three pitches (Operator, Smooth Revenue, AI Seating) and know which to lead with based on buyer signals.
- Run role-play scenarios: "The buyer says their biggest problem is slow Tuesdays" → Lead with Smooth Revenue. "The buyer says their tech feels like it was built in a lab" → Lead with Operator. "The buyer says they're leaving money on the table with their floor plan" → Lead with AI Seating.
- **Critical rule: The buyer's pain determines the pitch sequence. The rep does not choose.**

**4. Prepare Materials**
- One-pagers for each pitch (Smooth Revenue, AI Seating, Operator story) that can be handed to prospects or emailed immediately.
- ROI calculator or case study with concrete numbers (e.g., "Restaurant X saw a 14% lift in midweek covers in 60 days").
- QR code at the booth that links to a scheduling page for demos.

---

### During the Event

**5. The First 90 Seconds: Qualify, Don't Pitch**
- Open with curiosity, not a product dump.
- **Template:** "Hey, welcome. I'm [name]. What brought you to the show this year?" → Listen → "What's the biggest challenge you're trying to solve right now?"
- John does this well naturally. Sam needs to adopt this discipline.

**6. The Matching Framework: Pain → Pitch**

| Buyer Signal | Lead Pitch | Supporting Pitch |
|---|---|---|
| "Unpredictable demand / slow nights / can't budget" | Smooth Revenue (guarantee) | Operator (empathy) |
| "Our tech feels outdated / no one understands us" | Operator (founder story) | Smooth Revenue or AI Seating |
| "Leaving money on the table / floor is inefficient" | AI Seating (optimization) | Operator (empathy) |
| "Pain is outside reservations (takeout, POS, etc.)" | Acknowledge → Qualify out gracefully | Capture contact for future product fit |

- **Never lead with AI Seating unless the buyer's pain is specifically about floor optimization.** It's the most technical pitch and the hardest to land without context.
- **If the buyer's pain doesn't match any product, say so.** A 2-minute honest conversation earns more long-term trust than a 10-minute forced pitch. Capture their info, note the pain, and follow up when there's a fit.

**7. The 3-Minute Rule for Non-ICP Conversations**
- If within 3 minutes the prospect clearly doesn't match the ICP (no reservations, no seating, pain is entirely outside product scope), gracefully exit:
  - "It sounds like [X] is your real challenge right now, and I want to be honest — our reservations product isn't built for that yet. But I'd love to stay in touch because [reason]. Can I grab your card?"
- This frees up booth time for higher-fit prospects.

**8. Cross-Product Handoffs**
- If a prospect is a reviews app user or has reviews-related needs, DO NOT send them to a website contact form. Capture their info and make a warm internal handoff via email or Slack within 24 hours.
- **Rule: Every lead leaves with a human connection, not a URL.**

**9. Champion Empowerment (Don't Skip the Champion)**
- When the person at the booth is an influencer (not the final decision-maker), your job is to arm them, not bypass them.
- **Template:** "That's great — it sounds like [Decision-Maker] would need to sign off. The best way I've seen this work is if I give you a quick demo first so you can speak to it confidently, and then we set up a second call together. Would [date] work for the demo with you?"
- This respects their role, builds their internal credibility, and gives you two meetings instead of zero.

**10. The Close: Every Conversation Ends with a Calendar Hold**
- The #1 goal of every booth conversation is a **scheduled next step** — not "I'll send info," not "reach out to my assistant," not "I'll look into it."
- **Closing sequence:**
  1. Summarize the pain: "So it sounds like the biggest thing for you is [X]."
  2. Bridge to next step: "The best way to see how we solve that is a 20-minute demo. I can show you exactly how it would work for [their restaurant]."
  3. Lock the time: "I've got [Tuesday at 2 or Thursday at 10] — which works better?"
  4. If they resist a specific time: "Totally understand. Can I send a calendar link right now so you can grab a slot that works?" (Pull out phone, send it on the spot.)
  5. **Absolute minimum:** Get their email, send a calendar link within 5 minutes of the conversation ending, and reference something specific from the conversation.

**11. Lead Capture and Tagging**
- Every conversation should be logged immediately (within 5 minutes) with:
  - Name, title, company, contact info
  - Current tech stack (OpenTable, Resy, paper, etc.)
  - Primary pain point (in their words)
  - Which pitch resonated
  - Next step (scheduled demo, info sent, internal referral, not ICP)
  - Buying stage: Hot (wants demo this week), Warm (interested, needs internal buy-in), Cool (browsing), Not ICP
- Use the AI recording tool transcripts to supplement, but don't rely on them as the only record.

### Post-Event (24-72 Hours)

**12. Follow-Up Tiers and Timing**

| Tier | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Hot (demo scheduled) | Confirm calendar hold + send personalized prep email | Within 12 hours |
| Warm (interested, no demo yet) | Personalized email referencing their specific pain + calendar link | Within 24 hours |
| Cool (browsing) | Lighter touch email with relevant one-pager | Within 48 hours |
| Not ICP (but has other needs) | Internal handoff to correct team | Within 24 hours |

**13. The Follow-Up Email Formula**
- Subject: Reference their pain, not your product. ("Re: the Tuesday night staffing problem")
- Line 1: Personal callback. ("Great meeting you at the show — loved hearing about [specific thing].")
- Line 2: Restate their pain. ("You mentioned that [X] was your biggest challenge.")
- Line 3: Bridge to value. ("That's exactly what [Smooth Revenue / AI Seating] was built for — [one sentence of how].")
- Line 4: Concrete ask. ("I'd love to show you in a 20-minute demo. Here are a few times: [link].")
- No attachments in the first email. Keep it short. Earn the reply.

**14. Internal Debrief**
- Within 48 hours of the event, run a 60-minute debrief:
  - What pitches landed? What didn't?
  - Which buyer personas showed up? Were there surprises?
  - Pipeline created: how many demos scheduled, how many warm leads, how many not-ICP?
  - Rep-level feedback: what should each rep start/stop/continue?
- Feed insights back into pitch certification and pre-event prep for the next show.
