Conference Revenue Playbook

A repeatable system for converting in-person event conversations into scheduled demos and closed deals. Every recommendation grounded in 10 real conversations from the NYC Restaurant Show.

Executive Summary

The NYC Restaurant Show produced strong buyer interest but weak pipeline conversion.

From 10 conversations: 6 prospects were high-ICP matches, 5 expressed strong interest, but only 2 showed demo intent. This represents a 20% demo-intent rate — significantly below achievable SaaS event benchmarks (~50%).

The core issue was not product-market fit or buyer interest. Instead, three execution gaps reduced pipeline generation:

1
Weak closing mechanics
Reps accepted vague next steps ("email me info," "reach out next month") instead of scheduling demos on the spot.
2
Incorrect pitch sequencing
Product pitches did not always match buyer pain — AI Seating was presented to buyers who asked for predictability or partnership.
3
Champion mismanagement
Internal advocates were bypassed instead of empowered — turning enthusiastic contacts into reluctant participants.

If Soft Skillet improves lead-to-demo conversion from 20% to ~50%, the same event pipeline would produce ~2.5× more expected deals — without increasing marketing spend. This playbook provides a repeatable system to achieve that improvement.

Strategy Pillars

1

Target the Right Restaurants

Focus on operators with reservation-based service, existing OpenTable/Resy dissatisfaction, demand volatility, and multi-location potential. These restaurants convert fastest to Smooth Revenue or AI Seating.

2

Diagnose Pain Before Pitching

The highest-performing conversations began with operator pain discovery, not product explanation. Ask diagnostic questions, mirror the buyer's language, and select the pitch based on the problem — not personal preference.

3

Convert Interest Into Scheduled Next Steps

Most pipeline was lost at the final moment. Replace vague "I'll send you info" with a specific demo, two proposed times, and calendar scheduling on the spot. This change alone could double demo conversion.

Phase 2 — During the Event

First 90 seconds = almost entirely the buyer talking. Two questions:

"What brought you to the show this year?"

"What's the biggest challenge you're trying to solve right now?"

The power of mirroring: When Daljeet said "the rollercoaster is killing me," John reflected: "It's the uncertainty, right?" Daljeet lit up: "Exactly!" Before you pitch, prove you heard them.

Bad discovery: Brian said "we want a better partner." Sam responded with "AI-driven optimization engine that maximizes per-person-average." The buyer asked for empathy. He got a feature spec.

Brian Krakower: "I'm skeptical about taking control away from our hosts."

Wrong (what happened):
"It's definitely a tool for the hosts! It makes their lives easier."
Right:
"The system doesn't replace your hosts — it's a copilot. Think of it whispering: 'Table 12 might be worth holding ten minutes — high chance a four-top is coming.' Your host still makes the call. Still greets regulars, reads the room. We just give them better information."

Kelly MacPherson: "We've been with Resy and it's... fine."

Right:
"'Fine' is interesting. Usually it means the system checks a box but doesn't feel like a partner. It doesn't actively make your business better — it just exists. Is that close?"

Brian Krakower: "Changing systems for our size is a big operation."

Right:
"We'd never propose flipping a switch across 200 locations overnight. Our best partners start with a 3-5 location pilot, 60 days, real data from your restaurants. No risk, no disruption — you decide based on your numbers, not our slides."

Most common objection at events (Dee, Orla, Elizabeth, Karin).

Right:
"That makes total sense — you should see it first. Let's do a 20-minute demo for you, and if it's a fit, I'll help you build a summary for [name]. Then we set up a second call together."

Rep Battle Card

The step-by-step script for every booth conversation. Print for each rep.

BOOTH CONVERSATION BATTLE CARD

1

Qualify (0:00 - 1:30)

"Hey, welcome. I'm [name] with Soft Skillet. What brought you to the show this year?"

"What's the biggest challenge you're trying to solve right now?"

Listen. Don't pitch. Note their exact words — you'll use them in the close and the follow-up email. Identify: Are they ICP? Are they the decision-maker?

If not ICP → skip to Step 6
2

Diagnose Pain (1:30 - 2:30)

Mirror their pain back in sharper language.

"So it's not just the slow nights — it's the uncertainty. Not knowing if you should staff for 20 or 60."

This proves you listened. It also clarifies the pain, which tells you which pitch to lead with.

Predictability → Smooth Revenue  |  Vendor frustration → Operator  |  Floor efficiency → AI Seating
3

Match Feature (2:30 - 4:00)

Deliver the lead pitch in 60 seconds. Plain language only. Include one proof point.

"We guarantee a minimum number of guests on your slower nights. If our prediction is wrong, we pay you the difference. One of our partners reduced midweek food waste by 30% in the first 60 days."

If they're a review app user, acknowledge it: "Since you already know how we operate, I think this will feel familiar."

4

Handle Objections (4:00 - 5:30)

Address concerns. Layer a second pitch if relevant. If they say "I need to run this by [boss]" — arm them, don't bypass them.

"AI replaces hosts" → "It's a copilot, not a replacement"  |  "Current system is fine" → "Fine isn't great — what's missing?"  |  "Too big a switch" → "Start with a 3-5 location pilot"
5

Schedule Demo (5:30 - 7:00)

This is the goal. Every conversation must reach this step.

"The best way to see this is a 20-minute demo tailored to [restaurant name]. I've got Tuesday at 2 or Thursday at 10 — which works?"

If they resist → calendar link on the spot. If champion → schedule their demo first, stakeholder meeting as step 2.

No commit → confirm email, promise specific follow-up in 24 hrs. This is the floor, not the goal.
6

Not ICP → Graceful Exit (if reached from Step 1)

"Our reservations product isn't the right fit for that challenge. But I'd love to stay connected. Do you use our review app? I'll personally connect you with that team."

Capture contact info. Make warm internal handoff within 24 hrs. Never send to a website form. Max 3 minutes.

Advanced Conversion Tactics

Six tactics used by high-performing SaaS sales teams to increase deal conversion from event leads.

1
48-Hour Momentum Rule
All hot leads receive a call or demo within 48 hours. Event energy fades fast — every day of delay reduces conversion probability. Daljeet said "call me tomorrow." That means tomorrow, not next week.
2
Personalized Loom Follow-Ups
Send a short video showing how the platform applies to the prospect's specific restaurant. "Hey Frantz, I pulled up Rustik and wanted to show you exactly what Smooth Revenue would look like for your Tuesday nights." More personal than a PDF, harder to ignore than an email.
3
Champion Enablement Pack
Provide a concise internal summary that champions can share with decision-makers. One page, three sections: the problem, the solution, the pilot proposal. Dee needs this to sell Daniel internally. Orla needed this to sell her Director.
4
Restaurant Benchmarks
Include clear performance metrics from comparable restaurants in every follow-up. The NYC show had zero quantified ROI across 10 conversations. One proof point — "Restaurant X saw a 14% lift in midweek covers in 60 days" — is worth more than 10 minutes of features.
5
Referral Loop
Convert early adopters into referrals within the restaurant community. Daljeet, if successful, becomes a reference for every independent restaurant at the next event. Build the ask into the Day 30 review: "Who else in your circle deals with the same Tuesday problem?"
6
Pilot Scarcity
Limit pilots to create urgency and increase perceived value. "We're running pilots with three restaurants this quarter — I'd like Rustik to be one of them." Scarcity reframes the pilot from "free trial" to "selective opportunity."
