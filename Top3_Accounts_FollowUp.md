**Rep: Sam** (Conversation 9) | ICP: 8 | Deal Probability: 14%

**Why this account:** Tied for highest ICP score (8). Frantz is an owner-operator on Resy who is actively frustrated — "you're singing my song." He has relevant pain (vendor frustration, nickel-and-diming), full decision authority, and strong enthusiasm. The gap: Sam's conversation stayed philosophical (shared values, rapport) without getting to product specifics or a concrete next step. The follow-up needs to bridge that gap.

**Customer Signal:** *"You're singing my song."*

### What Sam Should Do Next

| When | Action | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| **Day 1** | **Personalized email anchored on his Resy frustration.** Reference the support black hole and nickel-and-diming. Lead with Operator pitch: "Built by people who've worked Saturday service." Include the anchor story (Resy pushed a mandatory update at 6PM on a Friday — broke his floor map, no human for two hours). Make the comparison concrete. He's already emotionally sold — now he needs the rational justification. |
| **Day 7** | **Demo tailored to Rustik.** Show the product through his lens: how Smooth Revenue applies to his slow nights, what the support experience actually looks like, transparent pricing breakdown. End with: "What would switching look like on your end? What's the timeline?" | Get to specifics. The booth conversation never got here. This is where the deal actually moves forward. |
| **Day 14** | **Migration plan.** Send a concrete Resy → Soft Skillet migration checklist. Offer a 60-day pilot with rollback guarantee. | Owner-operators need to see that the switch won't disrupt service. De-risk the decision. |

### Coaching Note for Sam on This Account

The Rustik conversation is a good example of Sam's broader pattern: strong rapport and energy, but the conversation stays at the values level too long without transitioning to product specifics or a close. Frantz was ready — he said "you're singing my song" — but left without a scheduled demo or even a concrete next step.

**What to do differently next time:**
- After 2–3 minutes of rapport, transition: *"Let me show you specifically what this looks like for a restaurant like Rustik."*
- End every conversation with a binary time choice: *"Tuesday at 2 or Thursday at 10?"*
- If they resist: pull out your phone and send a calendar link while standing there.
